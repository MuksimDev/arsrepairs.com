## Why Timely Microwave Repair Matters

Microwaves rely on high-voltage parts, safety switches, and control boards that must work together. When one part starts to fail, stress is placed on the rest of the system. Waiting too long can turn a simple repair into a full component failure.

A properly repaired microwave will:

{{< list >}}
Heat food evenly without cold spots
Run without sparks, burning smells, or arcing
Keep the door and safety switches working correctly
Operate without shutting down mid-cycle
Work quietly at normal fan and motor levels
{{< /list >}}

## Why Microwaves Malfunction

Over time, heat, moisture, and repeated door use wear down internal components. Electrical parts can weaken, safety switches can go out of alignment, and moving parts can fail.

Common causes include:

{{< list >}}
Weak or failed magnetron
Faulty high-voltage diode, capacitor, or transformer
Broken or misaligned door switches and latches
Turntable motor or drive coupler wear
Control panel, keypad, or display failures
Damaged waveguide cover or interior arcing
Cooling or airflow problems inside the cabinet
{{< /list >}}

We test each system carefully and explain what failed, why it happened, and the best repair option before any work begins.

## Common Microwave Issues We Fix

{{< list >}}
Microwave runs but does not heat
Sparking or arcing inside the cavity
Door will not close or unit will not start
Turntable not rotating or turning unevenly
Buttons not responding or display not working
Microwave shuts off during or shortly after start
Loud humming, buzzing, or grinding noises
Burning smells, hot cabinet, or tripped breaker
Error codes or intermittent power issues
{{< /list >}}

If you notice any of these symptoms, stop using the microwave and schedule service to prevent further damage.
